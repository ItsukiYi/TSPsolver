POSTER ORGANIZATION — Holistic Design Thinking
======================================================================

First, the most important decision: WHAT IS THE ONE THING a viewer
should remember after 30 seconds of looking at this poster?

ANSWER: "Hybrid classical-neural pipelines outperform either alone —
        and DIFUSCO→DualOpt proves it."

This single sentence dictates everything below.


======================================================================
THE CORE PROBLEM: Six Methods, Five Improvements, One Poster
======================================================================

This project has a LOT of content:

  Breadth:  6 algorithms (NN, Christofides, C+2opt, LKH3, DIFUSCO, DualOpt)
  Depth:    2 reproduced papers with engineering fixes
  Results:  3 benchmark suites (TSP-50, TSPLIB, scalability)
  Analysis: 1 ablation study
  Original: 5 improvement attempts (4 failed, 1 succeeded)
  Engineering: 4 compatibility patches

A bad poster tries to include ALL of this. A good poster makes tough
choices about what to CUT, what to FEATURE, and what to SUMMARIZE.

======================================================================
WHAT TO CUT, KEEP, OR FEATURE
======================================================================

★★★ FEATURE (must be prominent — these are WHY people should care):
  1. DIFUSCO → DualOpt pipeline (our contribution — the poster's centerpiece)
  2. TSP-50 benchmark table (the main quantitative result)
  3. "Complementary strengths" insight (the intellectual contribution)

★★ KEEP (include but don't dominate):
  4. Algorithm overview (compact table — shows breadth)
  5. Ablation: 2-opt is the dominant factor (surprising, actionable insight)
  6. TSPLIB results / generalization story (shows depth beyond toy benchmarks)
  7. Five improvements summary (compact table — shows scientific process)
  8. LKH3 as speed-quality ceiling (humble acknowledgment of ground truth)

★ SUMMARIZE (one sentence, or footnote):
  9. Engineering patches (impressive but not the research contribution)
  10. Runtime scaling details (the LKH3 dominance is enough, the full table
      can be a footnote)
  11. TSP-100/TSP-200 scaling experiments (per-scale training requirement)

✗ CUT (not poster-worthy):
  12. Full literature review (belongs in the report, not poster)
  13. Detailed training configurations
  14. Code snippets
  15. Individual per-instance breakdowns (use summary stats)

WHY THIS ORGANIZATION WORKS
======================================================================

1. THE CENTERPIECE IS VISUAL, NOT TEXTUAL.
   The pipeline architecture diagram at (5) is the poster's focal point.
   It's the one custom figure that tells the whole story: DIFUSCO makes
   a rough tour → DualOpt polishes it → best result. A viewer can
   understand the contribution without reading a single word.

2. RESULTS SIT IN THE CENTER, NOT OFF TO THE SIDE.
   The benchmark tables at (4) are in the middle column, where the eye
   naturally goes after the title. This is correct: the data is the
   evidence; the pipeline is the intervention. Evidence first, then
   interpretation.

3. CLASSICAL AND NEURAL METHODS ARE SIDE-BY-SIDE AT THE TOP.
   Sections (2) and (3) are adjacent, in the left column. This
   visually reinforces the "classical meets modern" theme BEFORE
   the reader sees the hybrid result. The layout itself tells the story.

4. FAILURES HAVE THEIR OWN SPACE BUT DON'T DOMINATE.
   Section (6) is in the lower-right — visible but not the first thing
   you see. The 4 failed improvements are scientifically valuable but
   should not be the poster's center of gravity. The pipeline (#2)
   should be.

5. THE BOTTOM BAR PROVIDES CLOSURE.
   After scanning left→right through the poster, the reader lands at
   the bottom bar with 4-5 takeaway bullets. They can walk away knowing
   exactly what to remember, even if they didn't read every word.

CONTENT BUDGET (per section, ~100 words max each)
======================================================================

Section ① TITLE BANNER:
  - Title (one line, bold, 36-48pt)
  - Author line (14-18pt)

Section ② PROBLEM & MOTIVATION (~80 words):
  - What is TSP (1 sentence)
  - Why algorithmically interesting (2 bullets)
  - The key question: classical OR neural, or classical AND neural?
  - Accompanying figure: TSP problem illustration

Section ③ CLASSICAL METHODS (~60 words + compact table):
  - Four algorithms: NN, Christofides, C+2opt, LKH3
  - Table: Method | Paradigm | Complexity | Key Property
  - Accompanying figure: algorithm_processes.png

Section ④ MODERN METHODS (~100 words):
  - DIFUSCO: diffusion on adjacency matrices, 5.3M GNN
  - DualOpt: divide-and-conquer, 710K RL revisers
  - Key difference: generation vs improvement
  - Accompanying figures: arch_DIFUSCO.png, arch_DualOpt.png

Section ⑤ KEY RESULTS (tables + 1 sentence each):
  - TSP-50 benchmark table (main results)
  - TSPLIB generalization table (compact version)
  - Ablation: "2-opt is the dominant factor" (one bar chart + one sentence)
  - Accompanying figure: pareto_frontier.png

Section ⑥ OUR CONTRIBUTION — THE PIPELINE (~100 words):
  - What: DIFUSCO tour → DualOpt revisers → best result
  - Why it works: complementary strengths
  - Key numbers: −1.67% vs GT, 24% more than 2-opt, kroA100 beats original
  - Accompanying figure: arch_pipeline.png (CENTERPIECE)

Section ⑦ WHY FOUR OTHERS FAILED (~80 words + compact table):
  - Compact table: #1-5, strategy, outcome
  - Insight: "TSP-50 is solved for DualOpt — revising input works,
    guiding internal process doesn't"
  - This section's real value: shows scientific maturity

Section ⑧ BOTTOM BAR (~50 words total):
  - 4-5 takeaway bullets
  - Key metrics highlight box
  - References (tiny font, 4 entries)

======================================================================
VISUAL HIERARCHY
======================================================================

What draws the eye, in order:

  1st → TITLE (top center, largest text)
  2nd → PIPELINE ARCHITECTURE FIGURE (right-center, largest visual)
  3rd → TSP-50 BENCHMARK TABLE (center, bold numbers)
  4th → KEY METRICS BOX (bottom, colored background)
  5th → Section headers (teal, consistent)
  6th → Supporting figures
  7th → Body text

The poster should work at THREE DISTANCES:
  • 3 meters away: can read the title and see the pipeline figure
  • 1.5 meters away: can read section headers and table bold numbers
  • 0.5 meter away: can read body text and footnotes

======================================================================
WHAT MAKES THIS POSTER DIFFERENT FROM A TYPICAL "BENCHMARK" POSTER
======================================================================

Most student posters: "We compared methods A, B, C on dataset X. A won."

This poster: "We compared classical and neural methods. Neither won —
they're complementary. We built a hybrid that outperforms both.
Four other hybrids failed, and understanding WHY they failed taught
us something fundamental about learned optimizers."

The difference: this poster has a GENUINE INTELLECTUAL CONTRIBUTION
(the complementarity insight) backed by POSITIVE AND NEGATIVE RESULTS.
It's not a bake-off; it's a scientific investigation.
